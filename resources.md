# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on the intersection of synthetic biology and carbon removal. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This is a genuinely interdisciplinary and fast-evolving field spanning plant/microbial biochemistry, process engineering, and carbon accounting — cross-check any specific cost, sequestration rate, or MRV methodology claim against current literature and standards bodies before an interview.

## Foundational Reading
- Erb et al., "Synthetic biology of carbon fixation" and related literature reviews on RuBisCO limitations and alternative carbon fixation pathways — good grounding for section 2's biochemistry.
- Bar-Even, "Formate assimilation: the metabolic architecture of natural and synthetic pathways" and related work on synthetic/alternative carbon fixation pathway design.
- The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) special reports on carbon dioxide removal for grounding in the natural carbon cycle scale and climate accounting concepts discussed in section 1.
- Major carbon credit methodology bodies' published standards and methodologies (specific bodies and their exact requirements evolve — check current, active standards directly) for grounding in the MRV concepts discussed in section 5.
- Companion **Synthetic Biology Designer** repo — read first if genetic circuit design, metabolic engineering, and biosafety fundamentals aren't already familiar.
- Companion **Bioremediation AI Engineer** repo — read first for the general environmental deployment, field-performance-gap, and data-challenge concepts this repo builds directly on.

## Key Application Areas (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- Engineered microalgae and cyanobacteria cultivation for enhanced carbon fixation.
- Enzyme-enhanced (carbonic anhydrase) direct air capture systems.
- Microbially-induced and bio-inspired carbonate mineralization for durable carbon storage.
- Enhanced weathering and biologically-mediated mineral carbonation approaches.

## Communities & Discussion
- Major carbon removal-focused research and industry communities and conferences (this field has grown rapidly and has an active, fast-evolving conference and publication landscape).
- r/climatechange and relevant synthetic biology discussion communities for broader discussion threads.

## Companion Repos (adjacent, complementary content)
- **Synthetic Biology Designer** — genetic circuit design, metabolic engineering, DBTL cycle, and biosafety fundamentals this repo builds directly on.
- **Bioremediation AI Engineer** — environmental deployment challenges, field-performance-gap concepts, and data challenges directly relevant to sections 2, 4, and 6.
- **Foundation-Model Scientist (BioAI)** — protein language models and ML-guided enzyme engineering concepts relevant to section 4.
- **Computational Biologist** — structural bioinformatics and metabolic modeling fundamentals relevant to sections 2 and 4.
- **Engineered Living Materials** — relevant background on biomineralization concepts discussed in section 3, there applied to structural material self-healing.

## Staying Current
- Carbon fixation pathway engineering, enzyme-enhanced capture, and MRV methodology standards are all fast-moving areas — recent journal publications, preprints, and standards-body updates are far more reliable sources of current state-of-the-art than any static reference, including this repo.
- Carbon market regulation and credit methodology standards vary significantly by jurisdiction and market (compliance vs. voluntary) and are actively evolving — check current guidance directly from relevant standards bodies and regulators for anything specific to your work.
